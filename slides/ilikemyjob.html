Data Pipeline

The data pipeline essentially takes data from disparate data sources and provides it to be queried. It is a lot like a water processing plant in your city, when it's working you won't even notice it is there, but it is still extremely important.

By providing this clean data, we are making the BIA's job possible.

Datasets

The Data Warehouse

What are Datasets?

Bespoke information generated for a narrow purpose.

Advantages

  • Easy to make
  • Quick to deliver
  • Fits the intended purpose

Disadvantages

  • Not always quick and easy
  • Maintenance
  • Stuck to a specific scope
  • Need to know all the columns at build time
  • Most datasets require little more than extracting from a source system adding a few columns and presenting it. Others are much more involved, such as our cohorts dataset. It requires several transformation stages, and nearly ten pages of documentation to explain.
  • They require lots of upkeep. Give example about survey data from QILT.
  • Not very good at being used outside their intended purpose. For instace, TM1 datasets.
  • You need to know exactly what fields the end user requires.

The Data Warehouse

So what does it actually do?

Takes data from disparate sources, cleans it, applies business rules and provides the data in a format which allows cross-source analysis and encourages exploration.

Why not just use the source systems?

  • Designed for reading and writing
    • Slow for massive queries
  • At the wrong grain
  • At the wrong time span
  • Lacks dimensionality
